Before you fill in the form

Gather the details that make a COSHH assessment usable.

A strong COSHH form depends on good source information. Start with the task, the substances, how exposure could happen, and what controls or emergency actions are already in place.

Inputs

Know the substance and task

Collect the product name, CAS number if available, supplier details, and the exact activity being assessed.

  • Safety Data Sheet (SDS) and label information
  • How the substance is used, handled, decanted, or disposed of
  • Amounts, frequency, duration, and who is involved
Exposure

Describe how harm could occur

Think beyond the substance itself: the route of exposure, the setting, and who might be affected all matter.

  • Inhalation, skin contact, ingestion, or injection risks
  • Who may be exposed, including cleaners, students, visitors, or contractors
  • Special risks such as aerosols, heating, mixing, or confined spaces
Controls

Document the real safeguards

A COSHH form is most helpful when it reflects actual practice rather than generic statements.

  • Engineering controls such as fume cupboards or local exhaust ventilation
  • Safe systems of work, storage, segregation, and waste handling
  • PPE, spill response, first aid, and emergency procedures

Good practice

Questions worth asking

Use these prompts to pressure-test whether the form reflects the real task and not just the template.

  • What is the worst credible exposure during normal work, cleaning, or spills?
  • Are control measures specific, observable, and already available in the workspace?
  • Could this task create vapour, mist, dust, fumes, or skin contamination unexpectedly?
  • Would someone new to the task know what to do from this assessment alone?
Suggested workflow

Move from source information to a finished assessment in four passes.

The tool works best when you use it as a drafting aid after collecting your source documents, rather than trying to discover everything while filling fields in one go.

01

Collect

Gather the SDS, identify the substance properly, and define the task, location, people, and duration.

02

Check

Use EH40 and PubChem as quick references for WELs, classifications, hazard phrases, and pictograms.

03

Write

Complete the COSHH form with meaningful controls, emergency actions, and disposal arrangements.

04

Review

Sense-check the assessment with a competent person before work starts and export the final record.
